What Your AI Employee Handles

Five Bottlenecks That Disappear

1

Intake That Sounds Like You

A prospective client fills out the contact form at 11 PM, scared, crying, unsure. Your AI employee responds within minutes in your firm’s warm voice — acknowledges what they’re going through, answers common questions, schedules the consult, and flags urgency (DV, custody emergency) for immediate attention. Nobody waits until Monday to feel heard.

Every intake handled with empathy in under 2 minutes
2

Client Communication on Autopilot

Court date reminders. Document requests. Status updates between filings. Follow-ups on paperwork you’re waiting on. Your AI employee keeps every active case moving forward and every client in the loop — without your attorneys drafting the same three emails fifty times a week.

10+ hours/week reclaimed per attorney
3

Content & Education Engine

Your site already has a library of free resources — that’s your best marketing asset. Your AI employee writes new blog posts, turns FAQs into YouTube scripts, drafts the monthly newsletter, and repurposes your expertise into social content that positions O’Connor Family Law as THE Massachusetts authority on compassionate family law.

4x content output, zero hours from your team
4

Reviews, Referrals & Reputation

You’re already Expertise.com and AVVO recognized — but reputation compounds. Your AI employee follows up with past clients at the right moment to request reviews, thanks referral sources personally, tracks which attorneys get the most 5-star mentions, and helps you double down on what’s already working.

Review volume grows without you asking
5

Case Triage & Attorney Matching

New leads come in with wildly different needs — mediation, high-net-worth divorce, father’s rights, relocation. Your AI employee reads the intake, matches each lead to the right attorney on your team (Caitlyn for high-conflict, you for complex custody, etc.), and books them directly on the right calendar. No more manual routing.

Right attorney, right client, first try
